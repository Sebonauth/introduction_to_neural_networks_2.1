Biological vs. Artificial Neurons

Have you ever wondered how your brain processes the vast amount of information it receives every day? Imagine your brain as a bustling city with intricate networks of roads and messengers carrying information. Just like these messengers, biological neurons transmit electrical and chemical signals throughout your nervous system.

In this lesson, we'll explore the fundamental building blocks of both the brain and artificial neural networks: neurons. We'll compare biological neurons—the inspiration for artificial neural networks—with their artificial counterparts. By understanding these similarities and differences, you'll gain insight into how computers can learn to recognize patterns and make decisions, much like our own brains.
